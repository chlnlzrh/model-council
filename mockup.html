What is the best height of the seat of an office chair in inches?
The best seat height for an office chair depends on the individual, but general guidelines are: Standard recommended range: 16–20.5 inches from the floor Most common ideal height: Around 17–19 inches for the average person The key principle is that your feet should be flat on the floor (or on a footrest), with your thighs roughly parallel to the ground and your knees bent at about a 90-degree angle. Height-based guidelines: • Shorter individuals (5'0"–5'4"): ~15–17 inches • Average height (5'5"–5'10"): ~17–19 inches • Taller individuals (5'11"+): ~19–21 inches An adjustable chair is ideal so you can fine-tune the height to your body and desk setup.

There isn't one single "perfect" seat-height number that suits every person, because the correct height depends on the user's leg length and the shoes being worn. The ergonomic goal is: • Feet flat on the floor (or on a footrest) • Knees at, or very slightly below, hip level (~90° bend) • Thighs roughly horizontal Popliteal height for the 5th–95th percentile adult: about 15–22 in. After adjusting for shoes (~1 in) and seat compression (~½ in): ≈ 16–21 inches from floor to seat cushion. If you need one figure for a non-adjustable chair: 18 inches is the usual compromise.

The Best Office Chair Seat Height There is no single "best" seat height that works for everyone — it depends on your body proportions, shoes, and desk setup. However, here are the key numbers: The Standard Range: 16 to 21 inches from the floor to the top of the seat cushion accommodates the vast majority of adults (roughly 5'0" to 6'2"). Single number answer: If you must pick one number for a non-adjustable chair, 18 inches is the most widely accepted compromise for an average adult. How to find your ideal height: 1. Sit with your feet flat on the floor 2. Adjust until your knees are at approximately 90 degrees 3. Let your arms hang naturally, bend elbows to 90° — that's where your keyboard should be 4. If your desk is too high, use a footrest Bottom line: Don't chase a specific number — adjust your chair so your feet are flat, knees at roughly 90°, and forearms parallel to the floor when typing. Start at 18 inches and fine-tune from there.
